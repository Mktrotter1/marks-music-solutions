# Qobuz Music Streaming Service -- Research Brief

> **Last Updated:** 2026-02-11
> **Status:** Researched
> **Verdict:** EXCELLENT ALTERNATIVE -- Cleanest ethical profile of all services researched. Highest artist payouts (~10x Spotify). Best audio quality. Audiophile-focused, music-only.

---

## 1. Ownership & Corporate Structure

- **Company:** Qobuz S.A.
- **HQ:** Paris, France
- **Founded:** 2007 by Yves Riesel (music journalist/entrepreneur)
- **Parent:** Xandrie S.A. (French digital music holding company)
- **Size:** ~50-100 employees (small, independent)
- **Notable:** Possible investment from Reservoir Media (NYC-based indie music company, late 2023/2024 -- verify)

### Political Ties
- **No known political donations** to any parties, PACs, or politicians
- **No ties to white supremacist movements, authoritarian figures, or extremist organizations**
- **No ties to authoritarian regimes**
- Small French company with no significant political entanglements

---

## 2. Artist Payouts

| Service | Per-Stream Rate | Notes |
|---------|----------------|-------|
| **Qobuz** | **~$0.03 - $0.05** | **Among the highest in the industry** |
| Spotify | ~$0.003 - $0.005 | Roughly 10x lower than Qobuz |
| Apple Music | ~$0.007 - $0.01 | Higher than Spotify, lower than Qobuz |
| Tidal | ~$0.008 - $0.013 | Higher than Spotify, lower than Qobuz |
| Amazon Music | ~$0.004 - $0.005 | Similar to Spotify |

**Qobuz pays artists roughly 10x more per stream than Spotify.** This is due to user-centric payment trends, higher subscription price, and smaller engaged user base.

## 5. Music Library

**Catalog:** ~100M+ tracks (comparable to Spotify)

### Strengths
- **Classical music:** Exceptionally strong with superior metadata (composer, conductor, orchestra, soloist, movement)
- **Jazz:** Excellent coverage and curation
- **Independent/European artists:** Strong
- **Audiophile remasters:** Unique Hi-Res versions sometimes unavailable elsewhere

### Gaps
- **No podcasts** (music only)
- Some niche regional content may be missing
- Available in ~25+ countries (vs. Spotify's 180+)

---

## 6. Ethics & Politics

### Positive
- Highest artist payouts of any major streaming service
- No algorithmic manipulation or controversial content promotion
- Active editorial curation by real music journalists
- French/EU regulatory environment (GDPR, labor standards)
- Digital download store supports music ownership

### Concerns
- **None of significance.** No scandals, no extremist ties, no labor disputes, no data breaches.
- Near-bankruptcy in 2015 (financial, not ethical). Successfully restructured.

---

## 7. Pros and Cons vs. Spotify

### Pros
- Best audio quality in streaming (24-bit/192kHz FLAC)
- Artists paid ~10x more per stream
- Cleanest ethical/political profile of all services
- Pure music focus (no podcasts, no algorithmic rabbit holes)
- Superior classical and jazz experience
- Editorial by real music journalists
- Download store with subscriber discounts
- GDPR-compliant, privacy-respecting

### Cons
- Slightly higher price ($12.99 vs. $11.99)
- No free tier
- Weaker social features
- Less sophisticated discovery algorithm
- No podcasts
- Fewer device integrations
- No lyrics integration
- Mobile app less polished
- Available in fewer countries

---

## Summary

**Qobuz is the cleanest option researched.** No political baggage, no corporate scandals, no labor controversies, no military contracts. Pays artists ~10x what Spotify pays. Best audio quality available. Trade-offs are a less polished app, weaker social features, and no free tier. Top pick for ethics + audio quality.
